Save the Leatherback Sea Turtle!

By Abbey Warke

Introduction

The Leatherback Seaturtle is a beautiful sea creature that is currently an endangered species because of high rates of commercial fishing bycatch, and marine pollution.

What you Should Know About Leatherback Sea Turtles...

  • They are 4 to 6 ft long
  • They can weight from 660 to 1,1000 lbs
  • They do not have strong jaws which is why they eat soft tissued animals - like jellyfish
  • They nest from 2 to 3 years and lay an average of 80 eggs - takes 65 days to hatch
  • The only sea turtle that does not have a hard shell
  • Have a wide habitat range and mainly found in the open ocean
How Many are Left?
Currently there is an estimated population of 35,000 nesting females. They were listed endangered by the U.S in 1970, but with pollution and high fishing rates the outlook of the Leatherback is not good.
